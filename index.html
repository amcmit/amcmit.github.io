About Me

MIT Center for Transportation & Logistics — Deep Knowledge Lab

I'm a PhD candidate at MIT's Center for Transportation & Logistics, where I develop and deploy machine learning and operations research solutions that connect forecasting with decision models to drive efficiency, resilience, and sustainability in global supply chains.

My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence and supply chain optimization. I believe we're at the cusp of an amazing transformation in supply chain research and the overall global economy—and I'm committed to being at the forefront of that change.

Before MIT, I held leadership and analytics roles at Amazon (Tokyo), Flock Freight, Zillow, and Arrive Logistics, where I developed pricing strategies, implemented ML systems, and led operations teams. My time as a site leader at Amazon Japan (Tokyo) gave me invaluable cross-cultural leadership experience and a deep appreciation for operational excellence.

Research

My research focuses on developing practical AI and optimization solutions for real-world supply chain challenges. I work at the intersection of machine learning, operations research, and industry application.

Agentic AI for Supply Chains

Exploring how autonomous AI agents can transform procurement, pricing, and supplier intelligence. Developing frameworks for multi-agent orchestration in complex supply chain decisions.

Demand Forecasting

Developing machine learning models for demand prediction in volatile networks. Combining gradient boosted methods with optimization for integrated decision-making.

Optimization & OR

Formulating and solving complex decision problems using mixed-integer programming, dynamic programming, and hybrid modeling approaches for logistics optimization.

Dynamic Pricing

Designing pricing frameworks that integrate market forecasting with optimization for cost efficiency and service performance in freight and logistics.

Current Focus
